1. The Main Organizations They Work Through

Registered agent companies do not lobby openly under their own brand. Instead, they work through influential legal and administrative groups, including:

OrganizationDescriptionWho Participates
International Association of Commercial Administrators (IACA)Professional association of state business filing offices (Secretaries of State divisions)Filing officers, CT, CSC, NRAI, Cogency Global, Capitol Services

2. The Strategy Step-by-Step

Step 1: Identify Inefficiencies

Large RA companies monitor state filing systems for delays, burdens, or missing Commercial Registered Agent (CRA) privileges.

Step 2: Draft “Model Legislation”

Through the ULC and ABA, they help draft laws like the Model Registered Agents Act (MoRAA), introducing ideas such as commercial RA status and centralized service of process rules.

Step 3: Soft Lobbying Through Trade Groups

They use IACA presentations, ABA comment periods, and published whitepapers to "educate" Secretaries of State about the benefits.

Step 4: Legislative Push

Once support is built, SOS offices or business-friendly legislators sponsor and pass the reforms into state law.

3. Real-World Example: Model Registered Agents Act (MoRAA)

Element IntroducedBenefit to Registered Agents
Commercial Registered Agent StatusBatch updates; no individual filing needed per client
Automatic Consent to AppointmentNo separate acceptance needed for every entity
Bulk Resignation RightsMass resignation allowed instead of entity-by-entity resignation
Standardized Service of Process RulesUniform lawsuit acceptance across multiple states

Without MoRAA and similar model laws, Registered Agents would face chaotic, varied compliance systems across all 50 states, increasing administrative costs and legal risk.

4. Important Note

While powerful, this strategy is:

  • Completely legal
  • Conducted through normal legislative and administrative channels
  • Framed as modernization and efficiency improvements for businesses
