Introduction

The Model Registered Agents Act (MoRAA), developed by the Uniform Law Commission in 2006, standardized how Registered Agents are recognized and managed across states, bringing efficiency and consistency to corporate compliance systems.

Why MoRAA Was Created

  • Eliminate redundant filings for Registered Agents operating in multiple states.
  • Simplify entity formation and service of process rules.
  • Enhance consistency in corporate compliance across jurisdictions.

Key Features of MoRAA

  • Commercial Registered Agent (CRA) designation.
  • Batch updates and bulk entity management.
  • Standardized service of process procedures.
  • Simplified agent resignation and address changes.

MoRAA Process Flow (Summary)

Company ➔ Appoints CRA ➔ CRA Registers Once ➔ Secretary of State Database Updates Automatically ➔ Compliance Streamlined.
