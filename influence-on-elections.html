Introduction

Registered agent companies do not directly fund Secretary of State candidates. Instead, they shape elections and policy indirectly through business groups, model law drafting, modernization initiatives, and policy research funding.

1. Influence Through Business Associations

  • Registered agents join chambers of commerce and business law councils.
  • Promote modernization agendas that favor batch filings and e-service technologies.

2. Model Law Development and Soft Lobbying

  • Influence ABA Business Law Section and ULC to draft model acts.
  • Model Registered Agents Act (MoRAA) heavily supported by RA companies.

3. Sponsorship of Modernization Conferences

  • Major RAs sponsor NASS and IACA conferences.
  • Promote modernization themes aligning with RA operational needs.

4. Funding Indirect Policy Research

  • Registered agents fund whitepapers on e-filing savings, compliance efficiencies, and service of process improvements.
  • These documents influence legislative agendas without direct political donations.
