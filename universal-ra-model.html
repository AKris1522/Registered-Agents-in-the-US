Introduction

The Universal Registered Agent (Universal RA) model proposes that a Registered Agent could register once and automatically be recognized across all 50 U.S. states, radically simplifying entity management and compliance operations.

What Is the Universal RA Model?

  • One-time registration through a national or multistate portal.
  • Automatic recognition across all participating jurisdictions.
  • One address update applies nationwide.
  • Simplified service of process procedures across states.

Why Registered Agents Support It

  • Lower compliance costs by avoiding repetitive filings.
  • Faster service updates and reinstatements.
  • Improved entity management efficiencies at scale.
  • Reduced administrative burdens for clients.

Risks and Concerns

  • Market dominance by a few large RA companies.
  • Loss of local control and oversight in some states.
  • Potential reduction in state revenue from individual filing fees.
  • Small RA firms may face barriers to competitiveness.

Progress Toward Adoption (as of 2025)

  • Early model laws under discussion at ULC.
  • Potential pilot programs in Texas, Utah, and Delaware.
  • Strong lobbying by CT Corporation, CSC Global, Harbor Compliance, and Northwest Registered Agent.
