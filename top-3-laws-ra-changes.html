1. The New "Universal Registered Agent System" Proposal (uRAA)

What it is:

An upgrade to MoRAA where a registered agent could file one centralized registration covering all 50 states, automatically recognized across state borders.

Why they want it:

  • Huge cost and time savings.
  • Simplifies cross-border company management.
  • Gives large RAs a competitive advantage over smaller players.

Current Status (2025):

  • Early drafts under review by ULC.
  • Test marketing through NASS meetings.
  • Potential early adopters: Utah, Delaware, Texas.

2. E-Service of Process Mandate for All Business Entities

What it is:

Registered agents want mandatory electronic service of lawsuits through the registered agent on file before allowing traditional physical service methods.

Why they want it:

  • Faster and more reliable service.
  • Reduces mailing errors and missed lawsuits.
  • Cost savings on physical paperwork handling.

Current Status (2025):

  • Pilot programs running (e.g., Delaware).
  • Heavy lobbying in New York, California, Florida.

3. Expanded Commercial Registered Agent (CRA) Privileges: Auto-Reinstatement Power

What it is:

Registered agents are pushing for the right to reinstate dissolved companies automatically without needing signatures, if fees and filings are cleared.

Why they want it:

  • Faster company reinstatements.
  • Creates new service offerings ("Reinstatement-as-a-Service").
  • Strengthens RA role as company custodians.

Current Status (2025):

  • Trial runs in Nevada and Wyoming.
  • Model bills being proposed through ULC and ABA channels.

Closing Insight

If all three initiatives succeed, Registered Agent companies will become even more critical to U.S. corporate legal compliance — transitioning from simple mail receivers to full operational custodians of business entities.
